WebGIS-Based Soil Fertility Information System in Cibodas Botanical Garden

The Kedaireka Project

I have designed and implemented a WebGIS-Based Soil Fertility Information System at Cibodas Botanical Garden. The system uses GIS technology for visualization of soil fertility data, allowing users to explore the information interactively. With a spatial database storing soil fertility-related data, the project provides in-depth analysis of nutrient content, pH, and other fertility factors. An easy-to-use, responsive interface that can be accessed from various devices, as well as real-time monitoring of soil fertility, are at the core of the project. Integration with external resources and strict data security add dimensions of complexity and usefulness to the system, supporting the understanding and maintenance of the environment at Cibodas Botanical Garden.

DEVELOPMENT OF FERTILIZER DOSING SYSTEM IN RICE FARMING BASED ON INTERNET OF THINGS AND GIS MAPPING

Capstone Design

In my capstone design, I led the development of an innovative system that combines Internet of Things (IoT) and GIS mapping to improve efficiency in fertilizer dosing on rice farms. The system aims to monitor real-time crop nutrient requirements, integrating IoT sensor data to detect soil moisture, pH, and soil temperature levels. Through a GIS mapping interface, this information can be clearly visualized and easily accessed by farmers. With a focus on automation and accurate monitoring, this project has the potential to increase agricultural yields and fertilizer use efficiency, while supporting sustainable agriculture. This capstone design reflects my dedication to bringing useful and relevant technological solutions within the agricultural sector.

Spatial Analysis of Flora Habitat Characteristics in East Kalimantan

Practical Work at BRIN

In my internship project at the National Research and Innovation Agency (BRIN), I led the Spatial Analysis of Flora Habitat Characteristics in East Kalimantan. This project focused on spatial analysis to understand flora habitat characteristics in the East Kalimantan region. Involving the utilization of GIS technology and spatial data, we investigated the environmental factors that influence the distribution and sustainability of various flora species. This research not only provides an in-depth understanding of local ecosystems, but also explores the potential applications of GIS technology in natural resource conservation and management. This project reflects my commitment to combine technology and nature conservation expertise to support sustainable development in Indonesia.
